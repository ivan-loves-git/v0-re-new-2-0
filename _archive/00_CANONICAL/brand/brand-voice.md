# Re-New Brand Voice

*Last updated: November 2024*

Our voice is how we sound across all communications. It's the personality behind our words that makes Re-New instantly recognizable and trustworthy.

---

## Voice Personality

### We Are:

**Expert but Approachable**
We know SME acquisition inside-out, but we never talk down to anyone. We're the experienced friend who's been there and genuinely wants to help.

**Confident but Humble**
We believe in our platform and methodology, but we recognize every entrepreneur's journey is unique. We guide, we don't preach.

**Professional but Human**
We take business seriously but remember there are real people with real dreams behind every acquisition. We balance expertise with empathy.

**Direct but Supportive**
We tell the truth about the challenges ahead while providing encouragement and practical solutions. No sugar-coating, no fear-mongering.

**Ambitious but Realistic**
We're building something transformative, but we're honest about what it takes. We inspire action while setting proper expectations.

## Language Guidelines

### Words We Use

**About Customers:**
- Entrepreneurs (not users)
- Acquisition entrepreneurs (when specific)
- Members (for community context)
- You/your (direct address)

**About the Process:**
- Acquisition (not reprise/takeover)
- Journey (not process)
- Platform (not tool/app)
- Guidance (not hand-holding)
- Framework (not rules)

**About Success:**
- Successfully acquire
- Close your deal
- Become an owner
- Build your legacy
- Transform your expertise

### Words We Avoid

**Overused Tech Clichés:**
- Revolutionary/disruptive
- Game-changing
- Cutting-edge
- Synergy
- Leverage (as a verb)
- Ecosystem (overuse)

**Intimidating Jargon:**
- Multiple arbitrage
- EBITDA (without explanation)
- LOI (spell out first)
- DD (spell out)
- Sophisticated investors

**Negative/Fear-Based:**
- Failure
- Risky
- Complicated
- Impossible
- Overwhelming

## Grammar & Style Rules

### Basics
- **Active voice** over passive ("We help you acquire" not "You are helped to acquire")
- **Present tense** for current state, future for aspirations
- **Second person** (you) for direct connection
- **Oxford comma** always (clear, consistent, and correct)
- **Sentence case** for headings (not Title Case)
- **Numbers**: Spell out one-nine, use digits for 10+

### Formatting
- **Bold** for emphasis (sparingly)
- *Italics* for subtle emphasis or quotes
- "Quotes" for direct speech only
- CAPS only for acronyms, never for EMPHASIS
- Bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- Em dashfor breaks in thought
- Avoid excessive exclamation points!

### Technical Terms
- Define on first use
- Provide context always
- Link to glossary when helpful
- Use analogies for complex concepts

## Voice Don'ts

### Never:
- Talk down to entrepreneurs
- Oversimplify the complexity
- Overpromise outcomes
- Use fear as motivation
- Sound corporate or stiff
- Be vague or evasive
- Hide behind jargon

### Avoid:
- Excessive enthusiasm (!!!!)
- False urgency
- Manipulative language
- Gendered assumptions
- Cultural stereotypes
- Age-related assumptions
- Success shaming

### Language Clarity
- Write for non-native English speakers
- Avoid idioms and cultural references
- Use international date formats (13 Nov 2024)
- Include country codes for phone numbers
- Specify currency (¬ EUR)

- Understand French business culture
- Acknowledge regional differences

## Voice Checklist

Before publishing anything, ask:

 Is it clear and jargon-free?
 Does it sound like Re-New?
 Is it helpful, not just informative?
 Would an entrepreneur find this valuable?
 Is the tone appropriate for the context?
 Have we backed up any claims?
 Is it inclusive and accessible?

## Writing Process

### When Writing:
1. **Know your reader**: Who are they? Where in the journey?
2. **Define your purpose**: What should they know/do after reading?
3. **Write naturally**: First draft like you're talking to them
4. **Edit for clarity**: Remove unnecessary words
5. **Check the voice**: Does it sound like Re-New?
6. **Test readability**: Would a tired entrepreneur understand?

### Quick Voice Test:
Read it aloud. If it sounds like something a helpful expert friend would say over coffee, you're on track.
