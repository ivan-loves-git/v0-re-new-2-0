# Re-New Target Audience

*Last updated: November 2024*

**Our customers are entrepreneurs seeking to acquire SMEs.** This document defines who we serve, their journey, and how we reach them.

---

## Primary Customer: The Acquisition Entrepreneur

### Who They Are

**Experienced operators and first-time entrepreneurs** ready to acquire and operate SMEs in the €1M-10M range in France.

### Demographics

#### Segment 1: Experienced Operators (60% of target)
- **Age:** 35-50 years old
- **Experience:** 10+ years in management/operations
- **Background:** Corporate executives, consultants, industry leaders
- **Capital:** €100K-500K available for investment
- **Location:** Based in France or willing to relocate
- **Language:** Professional English, working French
- **Career stage:** Seeking ownership over employment

#### Segment 2: First-Time Entrepreneurs (40% of target)
- **Age:** 30-40 years old
- **Experience:** 5-10 years professional experience
- **Background:** High achievers from corporate/consulting
- **Capital:** €50K-200K available
- **Mindset:** Ambitious but need validation and guidance
- **Stage:** Discovery phase, building readiness
- **Motivation:** Escape corporate, build something meaningful

### Psychographics

- **Primary motivation:** Ownership and autonomy over corporate employment
- **Values:** Independence, value creation, operational excellence, legacy building
- **Risk profile:** Calculated, data-driven decision makers (not gamblers)
- **Learning style:** Self-directed but value expert guidance and frameworks
- **Time horizon:** 5-10 year commitment to acquired business
- **Success metrics:** ROI, lifestyle improvement, meaningful impact
- **Personality:** Ambitious, disciplined, pragmatic, relationship-oriented

## Customer Journey with Re-New

### Stage 1: Awareness (Month 0-1)
**Trigger:** "I want to own a business"
**Mindset:** Curious but uncertain
**Questions:** Is acquisition right for me? How does it work?
**Platform Use:** Free assessment, educational content
**Key Decision:** "Am I ready to commit to this path?"

### Stage 2: Discovery (Month 1-3)
**Status:** Committed to exploring acquisition
**Mindset:** Learning and building confidence
**Platform Use:**
- Self-assessment tools
- Educational resources
- Market research tools
- Community engagement
**Conversion Point:** Subscribe to Explorer tier (€39/month)

### Stage 3: Preparation (Month 3-6)
**Status:** Building acquisition criteria
**Mindset:** Focused and systematic
**Platform Use:**
- Define acquisition thesis
- Build financial models
- Network with advisors
- Attend webinars
**Upgrade Point:** Move to Searcher tier (€199/month)

### Stage 4: Search (Month 6-12)
**Status:** Actively seeking opportunities
**Mindset:** Hunting mode, high engagement
**Platform Use:**
- Browse deal flow daily
- Set search alerts
- Initial evaluations
- Advisor consultations
**Service Use:** Book premium advisory sessions

### Stage 5: Evaluation (Month 9-15)
**Status:** Deep dive on 2-3 targets
**Mindset:** Analytical, risk-aware
**Platform Use:**
- Due diligence tools
- Valuation models
- Expert consultations
- Partner introductions
**Upgrade Point:** Move to Acquirer tier (€599/month)

### Stage 6: Negotiation (Month 12-16)
**Status:** Structuring and negotiating
**Mindset:** Intense focus, high stakes
**Platform Use:**
- Deal structuring tools
- Negotiation support
- Legal/financial partners
**Service Use:** Heavy advisory support (€5-10K)

### Stage 7: Closing (Month 14-18)
**Status:** Finalizing transaction
**Mindset:** Sprint to finish
**Platform Use:**
- Closing checklists
- Transition planning
- Document management
**Success Metric:** Deal closed!

### Stage 8: Post-Acquisition
**Status:** New business owner
**Opportunity:** Ongoing support services, referrals, success story

## Retention Strategy

### Why They Stay
1. **Continuous value:** New deals, fresh content, tool updates
2. **Community effect:** Peer learning and networking
3. **Switching cost:** Data, relationships, learning invested
4. **Success proximity:** "I'm getting closer to my goal"
5. **Habit formation:** Daily deal browsing, weekly activities

### Why They Leave
1. **Successful acquisition:** Mission accomplished (opportunity for new services)
2. **Give up on path:** Decided acquisition isn't for them
3. **Found deal elsewhere:** Competitive loss (learn and improve)
4. **Price sensitivity:** Can't justify continued cost
5. **Poor experience:** Platform didn't deliver value

### Retention Tactics
- Regular success stories to maintain motivation
- Continuous platform improvements based on feedback
- Exclusive partner deals and opportunities
- Peer cohorts for accountability
- Flexible pausing for breaks

## Key Insights

1. **They're investors, not job seekers:** Position as investment platform, not placement service
2. **Time is their scarcest resource:** Efficiency and speed are key value drivers
3. **They value expertise:** Will pay for quality guidance that saves time and reduces risk
4. **Community matters:** Peer learning and networking are retention drivers
5. **Success breeds success:** Customer stories are the best marketing
6. **Trust is paramount:** Credibility and track record drive decisions
7. **Education sells:** Teaching builds trust and demonstrates expertise
8. **Different speeds:** Must accommodate both urgent and patient acquirers
