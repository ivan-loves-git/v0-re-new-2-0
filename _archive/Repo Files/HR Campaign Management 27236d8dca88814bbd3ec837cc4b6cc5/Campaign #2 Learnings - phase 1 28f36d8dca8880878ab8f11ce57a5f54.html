Campaign #2 Learnings - phase 1

Feedback email to ICP 2.0 Team

Dear ICP 2.0 Team,

The application process for campaign #2 ended this week.

We collected 75 applications—a 25% increase versus campaign #1.

Most importantly, the quality of applications has sharply increased.

During campaign #1, ~22% of candidates were potentially fit for monetization with our partners.

Based on our current analysis, the ratio of potentially fit candidates for campaign #2 is at least ~36%. This number may increase after our upcoming interviews.

In total, we've received 135 applications across campaigns #1 and #2. Of these, ~40 require ongoing relationships to support them throughout their acquisition journey and ensure we take the right actions to monetize them.

We cannot do this properly without a clear understanding of what we want to achieve with our candidates. And we cannot scale without the right solution—especially since this ~40 number will grow as we interview ~40 more candidates with acceptable profiles and continue receiving spontaneous applications.

Key Learnings from the Campaign

  • Pre-scoring candidates saves time and helps us prioritize application reviews
  • We can do much more to streamline the process and ensure smooth application processing
  • We need to properly track key actions for each strong candidate after interviews—for example, connecting them with Partners X, Y, or Z, following up on introductions, and monitoring their progress

Outlook

  • We need to refine our recruitment process to maintain our high selection standards while spending less time on it
  • We need a clear process and solution to track all B-list candidates, maximizing their monetization potential while minimizing time spent

The better we manage this, the more candidates will successfully find their target company. This means more entrepreneurs we can financially support through our investment vehicle (in creation) and more entrepreneurs we can potentially support post-acquisition with our Operating Partner offer.

These are great steps—many more to come!

Thanks for your support
