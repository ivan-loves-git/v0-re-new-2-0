üéØ Presentation of the Re-New Process

Re-New is a rigorous selection program designed to identify and support highly qualified business acquirers. Our objective is to match experienced entrepreneurs with high-potential SMEs.

Process Objectives:

  • Select candidates with proven business credibility
  • Validate their ability to convince investors and partners
  • Test their managerial maturity and strategic vision
  • Ensure optimal matching with our partner ecosystem

üë• Your Role as an Interviewer

You are the guarantor of process quality and a showcase for Re-New. Your mission consists of:

  1. Objectively evaluating candidates' skills and credibility
  1. Challenging their project and motivations with kindness
  1. Collecting factual evidence of their experience and achievements
  1. Detecting inconsistencies between storytelling and reality
  1. Providing structured deliverables to enable decision-making

Principles:

  • Rigor: Systematically require concrete, dated, and quantified examples + reports
  • Kindness: Create a safe framework to reveal true potential
  • Objectivity: Base your evaluations on facts, not impressions
  • Collaboration: Share your observations with the team

üéØ Ideal Candidate Profile

Personal Maturity:

  • Good knowledge of their levers and limits
  • Fine understanding of the SME ecosystem
  • Capacity for introspection and clarity about difficulties

Business Credibility:

  • Proven and quantifiable experience
  • Ability to structure a complex project
  • Aptitude to reassure financiers and partners

Method and Vision:

  • Clear and documented project
  • Realistic and measurable ambition
  • Ability to engage a team
  • Coherent long-term projection

üé§ Interview Methodology

Essential Listening Codes:

  1. Require concrete proof
    • Always ask for lived examples
    • Obtain quantified results
    • Have them specify chronology
    • Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
  1. Challenge alignment
    • "Can you tell me about a deal you led from A to Z?"
    • "What tangible proof can you share?"
    • Confront storytelling with real experience
  1. Detect introspection capacity
    • Look for clarity about failures
    • Evaluate learning capacity
    • Measure humility and open-mindedness

Interview Techniques:

  • Open questions: "Tell me..."
  • Dig deeper: "Can you specify...", "What do you mean by..."
  • Benevolent confrontation: "How do you explain the gap between..."
  • Situational: "How would you react if..."

üìä Evaluation Grids and Sample Questions

Leadership & HR Questions:

Difficult decisions

  • "Tell me about a difficult decision: context, arbitration, short and long-term results?"

Project advancement

  • "What precise stage are you at? What are the concrete next steps?"

Team building

  • "What team do you imagine? Why would they follow you?"

Learning from mistakes

  • "What was your biggest managerial mistake? How did you learn from it?"

Business & Finance Questions:

Business Model

  • "Describe the business model BEFORE and AFTER acquisition"

Strategic resilience

  • "An investor challenges your strategy: arguments and plan B?"

Banking simulation

  • "Personal contribution? Financial coverage? Backup plan?"

Risk management

  • "Name the three main deal risks (excluding execution)"

Commercial Questions:

Growth levers

  • "Three concrete levers to triple revenue over three years?"

Go-to-market diagnosis

  • "How do you identify a commercial weakness in a target SME?"

üéØ Tips

Before the interview:

  • Read the complete candidate file
  • Prepare 3-5 specific questions based on CV
  • Plan 60-90 minutes without interruption

  • Take factual and detailed notes + AI
  • Challenge with kindness
