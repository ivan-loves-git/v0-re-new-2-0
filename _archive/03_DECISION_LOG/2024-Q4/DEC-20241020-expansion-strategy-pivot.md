# DEC-20241020: Expansion Strategy Pivot - From Italy Launch to Multi-Opportunity Exploration

**Date:** 2024-10-20
**Deciders:** Alex, Ivan, Piera, Gabriele, Nacho (team consensus)
**Status:** ‚úÖ Decided (Pending Bertrand validation)

---

## üéØ Decision

Re-New 2.0 will **explore multiple expansion opportunities** (geographic, vertical, partnership) rather than committing exclusively to Italy geographic expansion. The team will research and validate various growth vectors including Italy internationalization, family business succession services, and alternative partnership models before committing resources to any single direction.

**Approach:** Scout ‚Üí Validate ‚Üí Commit (not Commit ‚Üí Execute)

---

## ü§î Context

### Problem Statement

The initial ICP scope positioned expansion as **"Italy geographic expansion"** with the assumption that Re-New would replicate its French model in Italy, leveraging team members' Italian networks. However, this approach received critical feedback from Pierlorang (investor/board member) who argued the French operations are not yet stable enough to warrant international expansion.

### Background

- **Previous direction:** Focus on Italy as next market, begin operational setup in Q4 2024-Q1 2025
- **Team rationale:** Italy is geographically close, team has networks (Piera, Gabriele), language/culture similarities to France
- **Pierlorang feedback (Oct 2024):** "The project is not stable enough in France to expand internationally. You can work on expansion but from my experience as investor/board member, you may not be able to achieve much in the short term."

### Catalyst for Pivot

1. **Investor skepticism:** Pierlorang's critique made team reconsider timing and approach
2. **Competitor insight:** Discovery of German company "Cairn" doing ETA acquisitions + family business succession (dual revenue model)
3. **Business model validation:** Team acknowledged Re-New is still in validation phase, not scale-up phase
4. **Academic alignment:** ICP benefits from exploring multiple options vs. pre-determining conclusion

### Constraints

- **Time:** ICP timeline (academic deliverable by ~Q2 2025)
- **Resources:** No budget for actual international operations; limited to research/planning
- **Stability:** French operations still evolving (campaign model being refined, not monetized yet)
- **Validation:** Business model not yet validated (not profitable, still seeking product-market fit)

---

## üîç Options Considered

### Option A: Continue with Italy Geographic Expansion Focus

**Description:** Proceed with original plan to expand Re-New to Italy as next market, with Piera and Gabriele leading Italian market research, partnership development, and eventual operational launch.

**Pros:**
- Clear, focused scope (easier for ICP deliverable)
- Leverages team strengths (Italian members, networks)
- Geographic adjacency to France (cultural similarity)
- Concrete, tangible deliverable (Italy launch plan)

**Cons:**
- Investor/board skepticism (Pierlorang's concern: too early)
- French operations not stable enough to replicate
- Business model not yet validated (no proven playbook to export)
- Risk of premature commitment (learning from Italy might not apply if model changes)
- Ignores other potentially valuable opportunities

**Estimated Effort:** High (requires deep dive into one market)
**Risk Level:** High (commitment before validation)

### Option B: Pause Expansion Work Entirely

**Description:** Acknowledge Pierlorang's feedback by pausing all expansion work until French operations are stable and profitable. Focus all team resources on monetization and scale/refine streams.

**Pros:**
- Respects investor feedback fully
- Concentrates resources on core validation problem
- Avoids distraction from critical path (finding viable business model)
- Reduces team workload (Piera, Gabriele can support other streams)

**Cons:**
- Underutilizes Piera and Gabriele's skills/networks
- Misses ICP learning opportunity (expansion strategy is valid academic topic)
- Loses potential competitive intelligence (e.g., Cairn insights)
- May delay strategic planning that could inform current model

**Estimated Effort:** Low (no expansion work)
**Risk Level:** Low operational risk, Medium opportunity cost

### Option C (CHOSEN): Multi-Opportunity Exploration

**Description:** Expand the scope from "Italy launch" to "expansion opportunities exploration." Research multiple growth vectors including geographic expansion (Italy, other EU markets), vertical expansion (family business succession), and alternative business models or partnerships. Validate options before committing to any single path.

**Pros:**
- Addresses Pierlorang's concern (exploration vs. commitment)
- Leverages competitive intelligence (Cairn's family business model)
- Maintains optionality (can pivot if French model changes)
- Stronger ICP deliverable (evaluated multiple options systematically)
- Can inform current business model (e.g., family business might work in France)
- Uses team capacity productively (Piera, Gabriele contribute strategically)
- Scout-validate-commit approach reduces risk

**Cons:**
- Broader scope = less depth in any single area
- Could feel scattered if not well-structured
- Requires clear framework to avoid "boiling the ocean"
- Still may not lead to immediate operational results (Pierlorang's warning stands)

**Estimated Effort:** Medium (research across multiple domains, but less operational depth)
**Risk Level:** Low (research/validation focus, no operational commitment)

---

## üí° Rationale

### Why We Chose This Option

**Primary reason:** This approach respects investor feedback (don't commit to expansion prematurely) while still creating value through strategic exploration that can inform Re-New's evolution.

### Key Factors

1. **Business Model Validation First:** Team consensus that Re-New is still searching for validated business model. As Ivan stated: "The difference between a good idea and a good business is if you're making more money than you spend." Expansion should follow validation, not precede it.

2. **Competitive Intelligence Value:** Discovery of Cairn (German competitor doing ETA + family business) is highly strategic. Understanding what's working for competitors can accelerate Re-New's path to validation. Ivan: "If no one else is doing what you're doing, it's actually one of the biggest signs of risk... So I'm super happy that Cairn exists."

3. **Optionality Over Commitment:** By exploring multiple opportunities (Italy, family business, partnerships), team maintains flexibility to pivot based on what's learned. If French model changes significantly, Italy playbook might be irrelevant. But learnings about family business market could apply anywhere.

4. **Stronger Academic Deliverable:** ICP benefits from showing systematic evaluation of options vs. single predetermined path. Demonstrates strategic thinking and proper business process.

5. **Productive Use of Team Capacity:** Piera and Gabriele bring valuable skills and networks. Pausing expansion entirely would waste their potential contribution. Exploration mode allows them to add strategic value without premature operational commitment.

### Assumptions

- Bertrand (founder) will support family business exploration (Alex will validate)
- French model may continue evolving; flexibility is valuable
- Competitor research (Cairn) will yield actionable insights
- Team can execute structured exploration within ICP timeline
- Stakeholders (Francesco, Martin Kuh) will value multi-option analysis

### Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Scope too broad, team spreads thin | Medium | Medium | Define clear framework in follow-up meeting (Piera/Gabriele/Alex); focus on 2-3 key opportunities max |
| Bertrand doesn't support family business direction | Low | Medium | Alex to validate with Bertrand immediately; he's already interested per Alex |
| Exploration doesn't lead to actionable insights | Medium | Low | Acceptable for ICP (academic learning); focus on frameworks that inform future decisions |
| Still doesn't satisfy Pierlorang's concern | Low | Low | Exploration vs. commitment addresses his specific feedback; if validated, can execute post-ICP |
| Italy team members (Piera/Gabriele) feel Italy deprioritized | Low | Medium | Clarify Italy is still a strong candidate, just not the only option; their networks remain valuable |
